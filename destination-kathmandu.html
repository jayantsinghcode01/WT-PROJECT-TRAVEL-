About Kathmandu

Kathmandu, the vibrant capital of Nepal, is a city where ancient traditions seamlessly blend with modern life. Nestled in the heart of the Himalayas at an altitude of 1,400 meters, this UNESCO World Heritage city serves as the perfect gateway to both cultural exploration and mountain adventures.

Home to over 1 million people, Kathmandu is renowned for its stunning architecture, including seven UNESCO World Heritage Sites within the Kathmandu Valley. The city's rich history spans over 2,000 years, evident in its magnificent temples, palaces, and traditional Newari architecture that line the bustling streets.

As the starting point for many Himalayan expeditions, Kathmandu offers visitors the unique opportunity to experience deep cultural immersion before embarking on mountain adventures, making it an essential destination for any Nepal journey.

Key Highlights

  • UNESCO World Heritage Sites including Swayambhunath and Pashupatinath
  • Durbar Square with ancient royal palaces and temples
  • Thamel district - vibrant hub for travelers and shopping
  • Traditional Newari culture and architecture
  • Gateway to Everest and Annapurna regions
  • Rich Buddhist and Hindu religious heritage

Best Time to Visit

October to December: Clear mountain views, pleasant weather
March to May: Warm weather, rhododendrons blooming
Monsoon (June-September): Lush greenery but limited mountain views
